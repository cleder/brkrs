# Macuilxochitl

Macuilxochitl ("Five Flower") is a significant Aztec deity associated with art, music, dance, games, and excessive pleasures like gambling and drinking, leading the dangerous gods of excess (Ahuiateteo); he represents beauty, sensuality, and creativity, often linked to Xochipilli, symbolizing the soul's "blooming" and the sun's flower, appearing in art as a figure often emerging from a turtle shell or with flowers, embodying both divine creativity and potential ruin.

Aztec art often depicts Macuilxochitl with red skin, as having a human hand over his mouth, and carrying a sharp stick penetrating a human heart.

Macuilxochitl is God of:

- Gambling
- Games
- Feasts
- Tabac smokers
- Music and Dance
- Sacred ball game Nahuatl
- Palace Residents
- Voluptuousness
- Chief of the Ahuiateteo (gods of excess)
- Writing
- Painting
- Hemorrhoids
- Flowers
- Souls
